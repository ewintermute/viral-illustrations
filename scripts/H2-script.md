# H2: Treating Infection with Stool
*~220 words*

---

The cure for one of medicine's worst infections is stool.

I know. But stay with me.

C. diff — Clostridioides difficile — is what happens when antibiotics obliterate your gut microbiome and a toxin-producing bacterium fills the vacuum. Severe diarrhea, inflammation, potentially fatal colitis. The treatment is more antibiotics. Which is the exact thing that caused the problem in the first place. About 20 to 30% of patients relapse. For those people, each round of antibiotics yields diminishing returns. Some cycle through infections for years.

Fecal microbiota transplantation attacks this differently. You don't try to kill C. diff. You just repopulate the gut with a healthy donor's microbial community, which outcompetes C. diff for every resource it needs and restores the ecological balance the antibiotics destroyed.

The cure rates are 80 to 95%. For patients with recurrent C. diff.

A Dutch randomized trial in 2013 comparing FMT to vancomycin — 81% resolution versus 31% — was stopped early by the ethics board. It would have been wrong to continue withholding the treatment from the control group.

The FDA approved two commercial FMT products in 2022 and 2023: Rebyota, given rectally, and Vowst, a capsule you swallow. Pharmaceutical-grade, from screened donors.

Clinical trials are now running FMT against ulcerative colitis, cancer immunotherapy resistance, and metabolic syndrome.

The punchline turned into a treatment. Then the treatment turned into a platform.
