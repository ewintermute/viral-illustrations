# D2: The Cost of Dominance
*~240 words*

---

In almost every mammal, males are larger and dominant. The female spotted hyena is larger than the male, dominates him completely, and gives birth through what looks externally like a penis.

This is not a metaphor.

Female spotted hyenas have a fully erectile clitoris up to 7 centimeters long, physically indistinguishable in external appearance from the male organ. No external vagina. She urinates through it. She mates through it — which requires the male to insert through her clitoral canal in an act that is only physically possible with her active cooperation. And she gives birth through it.

That birth canal is approximately 1 to 2 centimeters in diameter, stretching to accommodate a cub whose head is much larger. Nearly 60% of firstborn cubs die during delivery. Often from hypoxia when the umbilical cord ruptures before the cub clears. First-time mothers face roughly 20% personal mortality from tearing.

Tearing permanently widens the canal. Second and subsequent births have far lower mortality. Evolution ran the math and decided it was acceptable.

The cause is prenatal androgen exposure: female hyenas develop in a uterine environment flooded with androstenedione, which converts to testosterone in fetal tissues. This masculinizes the genitalia and programs hyperaggression. The same thing that builds the anatomy builds the dominance behavior.

Hyena clans are matriarchies. A female's cubs inherit her rank. Males emigrate and spend their lives at the bottom of a different clan's hierarchy.

The pseudopenis isn't just anatomy. It's a social signal embedded in a dominance system. You need to be able to display erection to participate in the hierarchy.
