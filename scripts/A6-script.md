# A6: The Deadliest Animal That Ever Lived
*~240 words*

---

Sharks kill about 10 people a year. Crocodiles, a few hundred. Lions, maybe 200. These are the animals people worry about when they think about dangerous wildlife.

The mosquito kills 725,000 to a million people annually. Every year. Right now.

Stretch the view across human history and the number becomes almost impossible to hold in your head. About 108 billion humans have ever lived. Estimates suggest mosquito-borne diseases, primarily malaria, have killed roughly 50 to 52 billion of them. That's approximately half of everyone who has ever existed. One insect that weighs 2 milligrams.

The malaria parasite Plasmodium falciparum has been infecting humans for at least 5,500 years. We know this from Egyptian mummies, from ancient Chinese texts, from Hippocrates. It's shaped our genetics: sickle cell trait persists in malaria-endemic populations because carrying one copy provides protection against P. falciparum.

Military history reads differently once you understand this. When the British invaded Cartagena in 1741, 84% of the force died — not from the enemy, from yellow fever and malaria. During the Spanish-American War, mosquito diseases killed approximately 4,700 US soldiers. Combat deaths: 379.

In 1943, Nazi forces in Italy deliberately drained drainage systems and released Anopheles mosquitoes in the Tiber Valley. Regional malaria cases went from 1,217 that year to 54,929 in 1944. A biological weapon with no delivery system. No bomb. No visible weapon.

Malaria killed 597,000 people in 2023. Mostly children under five. One child roughly every 90 seconds.

The Guinness World Records certified it. The deadliest animal that ever lived.
